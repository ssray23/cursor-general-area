OLED vs QLED: Understanding the Difference

OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode)

How it works: Each pixel emits its own light - no backlight needed. Pixels can turn completely off for true black.

  • Black Levels: True blacks (pixels turn off completely)
  • Contrast: Infinite contrast ratio
  • Viewing Angles: Excellent - consistent from wide angles
  • Brightness: Lower peak brightness than QLED
  • Response Time: Very fast - excellent for motion
  • Burn-in Risk: Possible with static images over long periods
  • Price: Premium - typically £600-800+ for 55" models

QLED (Quantum Dot LED)

How it works: LED backlight + quantum dot layer for enhanced color. Still uses backlighting, so blacks aren't perfect.

  • Black Levels: Very deep blacks, but not true black (backlight limits it)
  • Contrast: High contrast, but not infinite
  • Viewing Angles: Good, but can degrade when viewed off-center
  • Brightness: Higher peak brightness - better in bright rooms
  • Response Time: Fast
  • Burn-in Risk: Minimal - not a concern
  • Price: More affordable - £300-400 for 55" models
Why No OLED in This Comparison? OLED TVs are superior for deep blacks and picture quality, but they're significantly more expensive. A 55" OLED TV in the UK typically costs £600-800+, which is well above the £350 budget. For this price range, QLED is the best option for achieving deep blacks and excellent picture quality.
Picture Quality Hierarchy: OLED (best, but expensive) > QLED (excellent, affordable) > Standard LED (good, budget-friendly). For your £350 budget, QLED models offer the best deep blacks and picture quality available.
